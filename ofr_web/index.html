I originally joined the Office of Financial Research digital media team as a writer. My company had been contracted to provide support in the development of the Office’s website. My job was to write copy where needed. The task turned out to be smaller than everyone had thought. I completed it in about two weeks.

Hoping to hold the position for more than a month, I found other ways to be helpful. Prior to launching the site, I oversaw the efforts to fix hundreds of content errors created in the migration to Jekyll. After site launch, I became the web producer, overseeing the publication of new content to the site. Since then, I’ve taken on other duties as needed. Some of my duties have included:

  • Editing pdf's to meet 508 accessibility requirements
  • Analyzing requirements for a CMS system
  • Developing a workflow for content publication in Jira
  • Preparing monthly website perforance reports using Google Analytics
  • Writing documentation and user guides in confluence
  • Migrating content from Jekyll to Drupal

After I became more comfortable coding, I took on some development projects. The most complicated project, was writing a node script for converting Bibtex files to HTML according the OFR's internal style guide. More recently, I rebuilt the senior leadership page, and built landing pages for the site's "Programs" section.
